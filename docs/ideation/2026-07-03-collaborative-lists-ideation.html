Collaborative Lists — Ideation

Design-iteration directions for the group climbing feature · 3 Jul 2026

Subject: the collaborative-lists feature (as built on feat/collaborative-lists) Focus: improve the design/UX Fleet: 5 frames → ~40 raw → 7 survivors Grounding: in-session (feature just designed & built)

The one thing to take away

All five ideation frames — pain, inversion, analogy, leverage, assumption-breaking — independently converged on the same verdict: the current design over-builds a persistent, hand-curated list living in its own tab, when the real moment is a few people standing at a wall who want the app to just tell them what nobody's sent yet. The strongest directions all pull toward: fold the group view into the catalog you already use, let it derive itself, and make it a live session rather than a saved object.

The seven survivors group into two coherent directions plus a sequencing call. They're largely complementary — you can pursue α and β together — but β contains a genuine fork (ephemeral session vs. keep a lightweight list).

Direction α — Surface & structure

The group view is the catalog, pre-answered and self-populating. Ideas 1–3. Lower risk, closest to your original vision.

Direction β — Session model

It's a live, ephemeral, co-located session, not a durable named list. Ideas 4–6. Bolder; contains the "do we keep a list at all?" fork.

Ranked ideas

1

The group view is the catalog — kill the separate screen & tab

where-it-livesConfidence: HighComplexity: M
Retire the Lists tab and the standalone "Browse & add" screen. In the normal catalog browser, a single Show for: me / us toggle recolors the existing rows with co-members' status and extends the existing status filters (Completed / Projects / Not completed) with per-member sub-chips. The group filter becomes a lens over the catalog you already live in, not a place you go.
Basis direct — this was your original vision; it got built standalone only to avoid destabilizing the 900-line catalog view. Every frame demanded it back.
Why it matters — collapses three surfaces (pile / group-browse / main catalog) toward one and makes an occasional feature cost zero navigation when unused (the toggle just says "me").
Tradeoffs — requires the catalog-view surgery you deliberately deferred; do it with the app running, incrementally, behind the toggle so the solo path stays byte-identical.

Converged from 5/5 frames (inversion #2, pain #6, analogy #4, leverage #3, assumption #4).

2

The pile derives itself — no manual "add"

the-shared-objectConfidence: HighComplexity: M
Stop asking anyone to hand-curate. The shared surface defaults to a live query over members' existing logbooks — "problems nobody present has sent, shared projects first." Curation becomes subtractive and optional (pin a few, hide a few), never the price of entry. The pile can never go stale because it re-derives from the data.
Basis reasoned — the whole payoff is a pure function of members' sent/tried sets, which the app already holds. Manual curation duplicates data you have and decays the moment someone logs.
Why it matters — deletes the "did someone remember to add it?" failure mode and the night-before homework; the feature works the instant people are together.
Tradeoffs — you lose deliberate "let's do exactly these 10" curation; pins recover that for the minority who want it.

Converged from 5/5 frames (inversion #1/#7, pain #8, leverage #4, assumption #3).

3

Open to the answer, not a query builder

finding-togetherConfidence: HighComplexity: S–M
The current per-member-chip × 3-bucket configurator makes the human construct the query (reason about "OR within, AND across") at exactly the moment they want to just climb. Invert it: the group view opens pre-answered — "nobody here has sent these; shared projects on top" — as a ranked feed. The boolean UI collapses to a couple of pre-baked lenses ("what can we all still work?", "who needs beta?") plus an "Adjust" escape hatch for the rare custom case.
Basis direct — "Not completed → everyone" is already the one query 90% of sessions want; making users assemble it every time is the friction.
Why it matters — turns a query tool into an answer. The clever combine-rule you built still runs underneath; it just stops being the user's job to operate.
Tradeoffs — power users lose immediate access to arbitrary member/bucket combos; the "Adjust" affordance keeps them one tap away.

Converged from 3 frames (inversion #3, pain #1, assumption #5).

4

An ephemeral "who's here" session, not a persistent named list

setup-and-joinConfidence: MediumComplexity: L
The boldest reframe (and the fork). Instead of creating, naming, and maintaining a durable list, you tap I'm climbing here at the wall; the app forms a session from whoever else is checked in on the same board right now. The shared view is the live union of everyone-present's projects/untouched problems. When people leave, the session evaporates — nothing to name, curate, or clean up.
Basis reasoned — the relationship is inherently ephemeral and co-located (2–6 people, ~2 hours). A durable named object imports a remote-collaboration model into an in-person moment; the Pokémon-GO-raid pattern shows co-located groups coordinate better around a start-now session than a maintained collection.
Why it matters — removes create + name + curate + manage-membership in one cut, and self-cleans (no "who are these 8 stale people in my list?").
The fork — pure-ephemeral loses the genuinely useful "plan tomorrow's session in bed" use case you designed for. Middle path: sessions are ephemeral by default but can be saved if someone wants to keep one. Decide this in brainstorm.

Converged from 5 frames (pain #3, analogy #1, leverage #5, assumption #1/#2/#8).

5

Session-scoped liveness — presence & live sends, not global realtime

livenessConfidence: MediumComplexity: M–L
You cut realtime to avoid the infra cost — but refresh-first quietly lies the moment someone sends. Resolve it by scoping liveness to an active session only: for the ~2 hours a group is together, sends propagate live (the problem greys out / drops off "nobody's done it" on every phone) and a thin presence rail shows who's here and pulses when they log an attempt. Realtime exactly when people are at the wall; nothing always-on.
Basis direct — tension #2: a friend's send doesn't appear until you refresh, so two people can unknowingly climb the same thing. external — Peloton's ambient co-presence rail creates "we're in this together" with zero chat.
Why it matters — the "nobody's sent this" claim is only trustworthy if it's current, and this is the app's first social feature — a little liveness is what makes it feel alive vs. a shared spreadsheet.
Tradeoffs — real subscription/presence work (Supabase Realtime); gate it behind the session so cost and complexity stay bounded.

Converged from 5 frames (inversion #5, pain #3, analogy #5, leverage #5, assumption #7).

6

Count only who's actually here — plus scan-to-join

setup-and-joinConfidence: HighComplexity: S–M
A real correctness bug, not just polish: "AND across members" over full, durable membership means one person sitting at home silently breaks the intersection — "nobody sent" quietly becomes "nobody of these 8, including the two absentees," producing empty or misleading results. Fix: a lightweight "who's climbing today" check-in, and default the filter to present members. Pair it with in-person join — a QR/short-code on one phone others scan — since the group is definitionally standing together (passing a deep link to the person next to you is the clunkiest possible step).
Basis direct — the combine rule applied to persistent membership degrades more the more people ever joined. external — Kahoot/AirDrop/Spotify-Jam room-code join is the native idiom for co-located groups.
Why it matters — without the present-roster fix the headline query silently gives wrong answers; scan-to-join removes the worst friction at the exact moment the feature lives or dies (arrival at the wall).
Tradeoffs — a check-in adds one tap on arrival; worth it to keep results honest. (Note: it also lets you scope exposure to this board, a privacy win.)

The present-roster insight is a unique catch (pain #2); scan-to-join converged from 3 frames (pain #7, analogy #7, assumption #2).

7

Sequence it: ship 1:1 compare first; let co-membership grow a friend graph

where-it-livesConfidence: MediumComplexity: M
A build-order and foundation call, not a screen. Ship "compare my logbook with one person" before N-way groups: it's cheaper, more frequently useful (partner beta, "what should we both try?"), sidesteps the badge-scaling problem, and de-risks the whole social layer. And record a lightweight persistent edge every time you climb with someone — a "people you've climbed with" graph that accrues as a byproduct, powering "start a session with Sam?" and any future feed, without an explicit add-friend flow.
Basis reasoned — this is the app's first social feature; the durable nouns worth investing in are people and derived data, with lists/sessions/filters as cheap views over them. The relationship data joining currently throws away is the classic compounding asset.
Why it matters — the group feature inherits a proven 1:1 interaction instead of inventing one at full complexity, and you lay track the rest of the social layer runs on.
Tradeoffs — "1:1 first" delays the group headline; frame it as the tracer, not a detour.

Converged from 3 leverage angles (leverage #1, #2, #8).
